Marina is a Lecturer at the Faculty of Geography, University of Bucharest,
specializing in remote sensing and Geographic Information Systems (GIS). Her
expertise lies in Earth Observation, with a strong focus on vegetation
monitoring and spectral analysis.

She works extensively with medium- and high-resolution satellite imagery to
investigate land cover changes, analyze vegetation dynamics, and explore
geomorphic processes.

Her research integrates multi-source geospatial data and leverages machine
learning techniques to improve environmental monitoring and natural hazard
assessment.

She has been actively involved in international research initiatives, including
Horizon Europe and Erasmus+ projects, contributing to studies on landscape
dynamics, climate change impacts, and environmental risks.
