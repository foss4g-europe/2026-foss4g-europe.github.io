### Getting to Timișoara

#### By Air

Timișoara is a budget-friendly destination, with many direct connections to
the main European hubs (e.g. London, Barcelona, Munich, Madrid, Istanbul) and
the Romanian capital, Bucharest. In Europe, the travel time ranges between
0.5-3 hours depending on the destination. Flight ticket prices are typically
in the range of 150-200 EUR for international flights and under 100 EUR for
domestic flights (provided that the return ticket is booked well in advance).
Companies that offer direct flights are: TAROM, Lufthansa, Wizzair, Eurowings,
HiSky and AirConnect.

The most convenient way to reach Timișoara is to fly to the Timișoara Traian
Vuia International Airport (TSR), the main aerial hub in Western Romania. The
airport is conveniently located at only 12 km away from the city centre,
making it quickly accessible within a short taxi ride (15 minutes). For more
information on the airport, please check the
[official website](https://www.aerotim.ro/).

Transport facilities include rent-a-car offices within its premises (e.g.
Europcar, Sixt, Premium Rent, Thrifty Car Rental, ExpediCAR, Alana, WLC) and
many others available en-route to the city). Otherwise, a taxi ride, including
popular ride-sharing options (Uber, Bolt), represents a convenient and fast
option. Typically, a one-way trip to the airport would be priced at 35-60 RON
(7-10 EUR).

For a more economical option, the Express line E4/E4b bus line connects the
airport to the city centre. A bus trip generally costs 2.5 RON (0.5 EUR) for a
60-minute ride. Tickets can be purchased with a contactless card, SMS, 24pay
or mobilPay app and also as paper tickets from STPT kiosks (paper tickets must
be validated on-board).

E4 line: express route Airport-Bastion (Aeroport-Bastion). Full schedule is
available [here](https://www.stpt.ro/en/linia-4.html). Useful for a ride to
the city centre.

E4b line: express route Airport-Barițiu (Aeroport-Barițiu). Full schedule is
available [here](https://www.stpt.ro/en/linia-4b.html). Useful if travelling
beyond the city centre and to the Timișoara Nord train station (North railway
station).

Multiple transport lines (trolleybus 11 and 14, metropolitan bus M11, tramway
2) connect the city center and the airport to the Timișoara Nord train station
(North railway station).

Other nearby airports with transfer by car include Belgrade (Serbia) and
Budapest (Hungary). Within Romania, the closest airports are Arad
International Airport (ARW, 60 km) and Oradea International Airport (OMR, 170
km). Other popular transit destinations with direct connection are Cluj-Napoca
"Avram Iancu" International Airport (CLJ) and Bucharest "Henri Coanda"
International Airport (OTP).

#### By Rail

The main railway hub in the city is the Timișoara Nord train station (North
railway station). Regional (InterRegio-IR) and international trains are
available, including direct connections to Budapest and Vienna.

Domestic routes to Bucharest are an 11h journey, while routes for Cluj-Napoca
involve a 7.5h itinerary. The total time for the
Timișoara-Budapest-Vienna route is 9h (including transfer in Budapest).

For railway information and ticket prices, check
[here](https://www.cfrcalatori.ro/en/).

#### By Coach

International and regional bus connections are available from various
Bus/Coach stations (Autogara). Flixbus and other coaches arrive at and depart
from Autogara Normandia.

#### By Car

Timișoara, located on the 4th Pan-European Corridor, offers convenient access
from Hungary, Serbia, and within Romania. Travel to Bucharest is possible via
the A1 motorway, a 7.5-hour journey that features the well-known
Transfăgărășan mountain road (DN7/E81).

### Getting around Timișoara

Timișoara is a walkable city, with taxi, ride-sharing and bicycle options
available.

In the city, public transport includes a network of buses, trolleybuses, trams
and vaporetto. Magnetic cards need to be purchased when using the network for
the first time at a cost of 1 EUR. Single tickets are valid for 60 minutes,
priced at 5 RON (1 EUR), and can be used for all motorized vehicles on all
routes. A day ticket on all routes can be purchased for 18 RON (3.5 EUR).
Tickets can be purchased with a contactless card, SMS, 24pay or mobilPay app
and also as paper tickets from STPT kiosks (paper tickets must be validated
on-board).

The vaporetto line, V1, transits the city on the Bega canal and represents a
unique way to experience and move around Timișoara. More information
[here](https://www.stpt.ro/en/vaporetto).

For official routes and times, the public transport company, STPT, encourages
the use of Google Maps and the Transit app.

Bicycles are available to rent through i'Velo, at 9 stations throughout the
city. The process is guided through the official i'Velo app, which allows free
rentals for a maximum of 2 hours.
